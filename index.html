Epistemological uncertainty quantification

The hard problem of data

We assume data represents reality. But every measurement disturbs what it measures, every label encodes a theory-laden choice, and every dataset has a generative process that shapes what can be inferred from it.

This is honest uncertainty: not just the statistical noise, but the uncertainty about whether your data means what you think it means.

Measurement couplingΔ_m
The instrument disturbs what it measures. A thermometer exchanges heat; a survey question primes the respondent; an fMRI voxel integrates over ~100,000 neurons. The value recorded is not identical to the latent quantity of interest, and the gap is systematic — it does not average away with more rows.
x_obs = x_true · (1 + b) + a + e
b, a shared across all rows  ·  e is per-cell noise
Labeling ontologyΔ_l
Labels are theory-laden discretizations. "Employed" means different things to the BLS, a welfare researcher, and a gig-economy economist. "Depressed" at DSM-IV vs DSM-5 cutoffs is a different construct. The label looks like a fact; it is a convention.
P(ŷ | x, L₁)  vs  P(ŷ | x, L₂)
Δ_l = spread over defensible label sets L
Generative processΔ_s
Who appears in the data, and why? Participation is never random — it reflects platform, incentive, reachability, time, framing. The mechanism produces P_data(X,Y), which may diverge from P_target in ways you cannot see from inside the sample.
Δ_s = D( P_data ‖ P_target )
often unidentifiable without external knowledge
Model uncertaintyΔ_θ
Classical epistemic and aleatoric uncertainty — parameter posteriors, conformal intervals, ensembles. This is what error bars usually report. In this framework it is the innermost layer, not the only one.
Δ_θ = Var[ ŷ ] under the posterior / bootstrap

The slider math here is a didactic proxy — strata compound multiplicatively as EUQ = 1 − ∏(1 − Δᵢ). The Python library does the real work: a nested Monte-Carlo propagation that perturbs features, labels, weights, and rows through your actual estimand and reports a per-stratum variance decomposition.
